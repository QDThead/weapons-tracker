## What Was Built This Session

### 1. Cobalt Demand vs Supply Sufficiency UI (3D Globe Detail Panel)

**Spec:** `docs/superpowers/specs/2026-03-29-cobalt-sufficiency-design.md`
**Plan:** `docs/superpowers/plans/2026-03-29-cobalt-sufficiency.md`

Added an interactive sufficiency analysis row to the Cobalt globe detail panel (front and center, right below the header):
- **Radial supply gauge** — speedometer showing supply/demand ratio (red/amber/green zones)
- **Platform dependency cards** — 16 CAF platforms split into 🔗 INDIRECT (through foreign OEMs like P&W, GE) and 🍁 DIRECT (domestic consumption), each with dependency chain breadcrumbs
- **Continuous scenario slider** — 0-100% disruption with 5 preset snap points (Normal → China ban → China+DRC collapse → Defence priority → Canada sovereign)
- **COA toggle** — 6 recommended courses of action with cost/impact, relevance-highlighted by scenario
- **Shipping routes menu** — now collapsible with toggle arrow

**Key insight this UI communicates:** Canada's cobalt vulnerability is supply chain architecture, not volume. 54% of demand flows through foreign OEMs.

**Data added to `mineral_supply_chains.py`:**
- 16 platform demand entries (10 indirect, 6 direct) with OEM, engine, alloy, and threshold_ratio
- 5 disruption scenarios with supply/demand/ratio/verdict
- 6 COAs with cost, impact, and relevant scenario positions
- Totals: 298 kg/yr steady-state, 740 kg/yr during F-35 ramp

**Tests:** 9 new tests in `tests/test_globe.py` → `TestCobaltSufficiency` class. All 23 globe tests pass.
