Question 2: Supply Chain Illumination & Network Mapping 
Requirement: How does your solution define supply chain illumination and/or supply chain 
network mapping?

Quantum Data Technologies defines Supply Chain Illumination not merely as a static list of 
suppliers, but as the automated construction of a dynamic "Multi-Layer Knowledge Graph" 
that maps relationships beyond Tier 1. 
 
Our PSI solution illuminates the network across three distinct dimensions to provide true "Rocks 
to Rockets" visibility: 
1. The Corporate Graph (The "Who") 
- 
Definition: Mapping the legal and financial relationships between entities to identify 
control and dependency. 
- 
Capability: PSI ingests corporate registry data, Dun & Bradstreet feeds, and proprietary 
sources to map Ultimate Beneficial Ownership (UBO). 
- 
Defence Application: This allows DND to trace a Tier 3 tactical radio component 
manufacturer back to its parent company. If that parent company is acquired by a hostile 
state actor (FOCI Risk), the "Illumination" engine flags the entire branch of the supply 
chain as compromised. 
2. The Physical Graph (The "Where") 
- 
Definition: Mapping the kinetic movement of goods through the global logistics 
infrastructure. 
- 
Capability: Leveraging our World Trade Model technology (previously deployed at the 
Office of Naval Intelligence), we ingest Bill of Lading (BoL) data, AIS vessel tracking, 
and customs records to map the actual physical path of materials. 
- 
Defence Application: We illuminate the "Logistical Chokepoints" (e.g., the Strait of 
Hormuz, Suez Canal, or a specific port terminal) that connect raw critical minerals 
("Rocks") to Canadian manufacturing hubs. 
3. The Risk Propagation Graph (The "What If") 
- 
Definition: Modeling how a disruption at a lower node cascades upstream to affect the 
final mission. 
- 
Capability: PSI utilizes "Dependency Logic". It defines the network as a directed graph 
where nodes (suppliers/factories) are linked by edges (transport routes). 
- 
Defence Application: If a Tier 4 microchip factory in Taiwan goes offline (e.g., due to a 
typhoon), the system uses Network Risk Propagation to calculate which Tier 1 Weapon 
Systems will face delays in 6 months.

Summary: For QDT, "Illumination" is the continuous, AI-driven process of linking Corporate 
Identity (Ownership) with Physical Activity (Trade Flows) to expose hidden vulnerabilities in 
the sub-tier network.

To achieve true "Rocks to Rockets" resilience, DND requires visibility beyond the direct Tier 1

Graph (from our work with the Office of Naval Intelligence). 
 
Our solution illuminates the supply chain depth through three distinct methods: 
1. Physical Trade Reconstruction (The "Rocks" Layer) 
Unlike standard risk tools that rely on survey data, QDT utilizes the World Trade Model

Defensive Value: This allows DND to identify if a Tier 1 Canadian supplier is

We do not just map who makes the part; we map how it arrives. 
 
- 
Vessel & Route Attribution: Leveraging our maritime intelligence capabilities, we map

- 
Chokepoint Monitoring: We monitor strategic passages (e.g., Suez Canal, Strait of

Impact Modeling: If a chokepoint is disrupted (e.g., by blockage or geopolitical

Inferred Mapping: Even without direct data from a Tier 1 supplier, PSI uses historical

- 
Propagation Algorithms: A risk event at Tier 4 (e.g., a fire at a sub-component factory)

Question 4: Item-Based Illumination 
Requirement: How does your solution allow for supply chain illumination/network mapping 
using items rather than entities (e.g., ships, ammunition, components, raw materials)?

DND/CAF operations run on materiel, not just contracts. QDT's PSI platform distinguishes itself 
by utilizing an Item-Centric Data Model. While we map Entities (Companies), we also map the 
"Items" (Goods) that flow between them using a hierarchical "Bill of Materials" (BOM) logic. 
 
Our solution illuminates the "Item Chain" across three levels of granularity: 
1. The "Rocket" Level: Defence Materiel (Finished Systems) 
- 
Ingestion: PSI ingests DND's internal catalog data (NSNs - NATO Stock Numbers) and 
vendor BOMs to create a digital twin of the finished good (e.g., a Frigate or 155mm 
Ammunition). 
- 
Capability: We map the finished item to its "Parent" entity (the Prime Contractor). 
- 
Risk Application: If a Prime Contractor is flagged for financial distress, PSI instantly 
lists every "Critical Item" (by NSN) associated with that vendor, allowing the DSCRO to 
identify which capabilities are at risk of being undelivered. 
2. The "Component" Level: Sub-Assemblies & Parts 
- 
Explosion: PSI supports "BOM Explosion". We map the finished item down to its 
critical sub-components (e.g., circuit boards, guidance chips, landing gear). 
- 
Multi-Sourcing Analysis: We identify if a specific component (e.g., a specific GPU) is 
used across multiple different weapon systems. 
- 
Risk Application: If a specific sub-tier manufacturer of circuit boards faces a cyber 
attack, PSI highlights every finished system (Drone, Radio, Missile) that relies on that 
specific board, even if they are made by different Primes. 
3. The "Rock" Level: Raw & Refined Materials 
- 
Commodity Tracking: This is a unique QDT differentiator. We track items at the HS 
Code (Harmonized System) and SITC Code level. 
- 
Provenance Tracing: We map the flow of raw inputs - such as Lithium, Cobalt, or 
Chemicals - from their country of origin. 
- 
Risk Application: 
- 
Sanctions Checks: We identify if the "Titanium" in a landing gear originated 
from a sanctioned Russian smelter, even if it was processed through three 
intermediary countries. 
- 
Inferred Dependency: Even if DND does not possess the full sub-tier BOM, PSI 
uses "Inferred Logic". (e.g., "This battery usually requires Cobalt; 60% of world 
Cobalt moves through Port X; Port X is blocked. Therefore, this Battery is at 
risk.")

Summary: QDT does not just tell you who you are buying from; we tell you what you are 
buying and what it is made of. We link the Raw Material (Rock) → Component → Finished 
System (Rocket) into a single unified risk graph.

Question 8: Data Integrity & Confidence Testing 
Requirement: What is the confidence-testing regime your solution uses to ensure integrity, 
validity, and accuracy of data and analyses? Are third parties involved in this process? 
 
QDT operates on a "Zero Trust / Glass Box" data philosophy. We recognize that in a defence 
context, AI cannot be a "Black Box" arbiter of truth. 
 
Our regime consists of three distinct validation layers: 
Layer 1: Automated Source Cross-Validation (The "Triangulation" Protocol) 
PSI never relies on a single data point to trigger a high-impact alert. 
 
- 
Methodology: The system uses Triangulation Logic. If a risk signal (e.g., "Supplier 
Bankruptcy") is detected from Source A (e.g., a news scraper), the system automatically 
queries Sources B and C (e.g., Court Filings and Credit Rating feeds) to corroborate.

- 
Confidence Scoring: Every insight is assigned a Confidence Score (0-100%): 
- 
Low Confidence (<50%): Unverified single-source rumor 
- 
Medium Confidence (50-90%): Partially corroborated 
- 
High Confidence (>90%): Corroborated by 3+ independent sources 
Layer 2: Model Performance Monitoring (The "Drift" Check) 
Our analytical models are continuously audited for accuracy. 
 
- 
Champion vs. Challenger: We run nightly tests where model predictions are compared 
against actual outcomes. 
- 
Drift Detection: If a model's accuracy drops below threshold (<85%), it is automatically 
flagged for recalibration. 
- 
Audit Trail: All model versions and performance metrics are logged for compliance 
review. 
Layer 3: "Glass Box" Lineage & Human Adjudication 
We provide DND officers with the "Evidence Locker" required to make their own determination. 
 
- 
Source Transparency: When PSI flags a risk, it presents the Source Lineage: the 
specific corporate registry document, the specific news article (with translation), or the 
exact Bill of Lading and is visually mapped in a digital twin of the world. 
- 
Adjudication Workflow: DND Analysts can review evidence and mark findings as 
"Verified" or "False Positive." This feedback retrains the system (Reinforcement 
Learning from Human Feedback). 
Third-Party Involvement 
- 
Data Providers: Third-party data providers (e.g., Dun & Bradstreet, credit rating 
agencies) have their own quality assurance processes. QDT validates data against 
multiple providers. 
- 
Penetration Testing: Annual third-party security assessments include data integrity 
verification if required for this contract. 
 
Summary: Our regime ensures that DND decision-makers verify facts, not just consume 
outputs. By providing full Data Lineage and Source Triangulation, we reduce validation time 
from hours to minutes.
